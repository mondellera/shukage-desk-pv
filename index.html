WHY SHADOWING

Shadowing — listening to spoken language and echoing it in real time — is, by recent accounts, gaining recognition in cognitive science as an effective way to develop a natural feel for a language. Once known mainly as training for simultaneous interpreters, it has found a wider audience as a path to language immersion.

ShuKage brings shadowing practice directly into YouTube, the world's richest source of authentic spoken language. Whether you want to truly touch a new language rather than formally study it — or you're an interpreter looking for spare-moment practice — ShuKage is built for immersion at your own pace.

HOW TO USE
01
Open a YouTube video
Navigate to any YouTube watch page in your browser. ShuKage activates automatically.
02
Click the ShuKage icon
Click the extension icon in the toolbar to open the side panel. A control bar appears at the bottom of the video.
03
Set A and B points
While the video plays, click < to set point A and > for point B. Press ADD to save the segment as a marker. The video is added to your library automatically if not already registered.
04
Loop and shadow
Enable A-B loop. Listen once, then speak along. Recording starts automatically when playback begins and stops when the segment ends.
05
Review and repeat
Open the WaveScope to compare your recordings. Rate each marker (difficulty, familiarity, and more) and track your progress in Stats.
KEY FEATURES
LIBRARY
Organize content by language, markers, tags, and difficulty. Import/export keeps your data manageable as your collection grows.
MARKERS
Define any A-B segment in a video. Attach names, ratings, tags, notes, and recordings to each moment.
A-B LOOP
Repeat any segment with configurable pause time between iterations — your shadow window.
RECORDING
Capture your voice to check pronunciation and conversation rhythm. All audio stays on your device.
WAVESCOPE
Review recordings as waveforms. Stack multiple takes for side-by-side comparison.
AUTO-RECORD MODE
Recording starts with playback and stops automatically after a set delay when the video pauses.
TIMELINE BAR
Visual marker overlay on the video timeline for instant navigation and at-a-glance context.
STATS
Track practice history, recording volume, and rating trends (difficulty, familiarity) over time with charts.

NOTES & KNOWN BEHAVIOR
AFTER UPDATING
After updating the extension, reload any open YouTube tabs to ensure the latest version is active. If the control bar disappears or becomes unresponsive, reloading the page will restore it.
SIDE PANEL BEHAVIOR
When switching tabs or using the browser back button, the icon state may occasionally differ from the actual on/off state. This is a browser limitation. Opening a YouTube video and clicking the icon will restore the correct state.
NON-YOUTUBE TABS
ShuKage automatically turns off when you navigate away from a YouTube video page. To re-enable, navigate to a YouTube video and click the extension icon.
BRAVE BROWSER
On Brave, the side panel occupies screen width which may cause YouTube to hide the video title and description in normal view. Switching to YouTube's wide or theater mode resolves this.
TIPS
RECORDING: FILE FORMAT

Chrome records audio in WebM container with Opus codec — a browser standard. The sample rate is determined by your OS audio settings (typically 48 kHz). WebM/Opus offers excellent quality at small file sizes and is widely supported in modern browsers and media players.


RECORDING: CONVERTING WITH FFMPEG

If you need MP3, WAV, or M4A, ffmpeg is a free command-line tool that converts audio formats easily.



# WebM → MP3
ffmpeg -i recording.webm output.mp3

# WebM → WAV (lossless)
ffmpeg -i recording.webm output.wav

# WebM → M4A
ffmpeg -i recording.webm -c:a aac output.m4a
