What is ShuKage?

ShuKage is a Chrome extension for shadowing practice on YouTube. Shadowing means listening to a short audio segment and speaking (or recording yourself speaking) in sync with the speaker, which is an effective technique for pronunciation and fluency in language learning.

ShuKage lets you mark any section of a YouTube video as a marker, loop it, record your voice over it, and compare your recording with the original — all without leaving the page.

02

Basic workflow

The typical flow from opening a video to completing a shadowing session:

Open a YouTube video Navigate to any YouTube video. You don't need to add it to the library first — if you set A/B and press ADD, the video and marker are registered together automatically.
To add a video without a marker (e.g. to browse it later), click the ＋ button in the sidebar header while the video is playing.
Set the A and B points Play the video to where you want the loop to start, then click < to set point A. Do the same for the end point with > for B.
The A and B label buttons seek to the saved marker position (double-click to also play from there). They are not used for setting points.
Add a marker Click ADD to save the A–B range as a marker. The marker appears in the sidebar tree under the video.
Select the marker and listen a few times Click the marker in the tree to select it. Use the loop button ⟳ to loop the segment. Listen carefully before attempting to shadow.
Record shadowing with auto-playback This is the main recording mode. Click the loop play button to start the segment. The extension automatically starts recording your microphone when playback begins. When playback reaches point B, playback stops; recording continues for the selected rec-stop gap, then stops and saves automatically. The default gap is 2 seconds, so you don't need to press stop yourself.
In auto-playback recording, stopping playback before point B also stops recording after the selected rec-stop gap.
Review your recording Open the marker properties by clicking the marker in the tree. Your recordings appear as waveform cards: use ▶ to play a recording, click its start time to seek the video, or open WaveScope to overlay and play back multiple recordings together.

Recording modes

ShuKage has two ways to record. See the control-bar buttons above for exact behavior.

Auto-playback The main mode (▶⏺). Recording starts and stops together with playback, so you just play a segment and speak along. Works seamlessly with the AB loop. Recommended for most shadowing sessions.
Manual The ⏺ button starts recording immediately, independent of playback, and you stop it the same way. Useful for free-form recording outside a defined A–B segment.

Known issues & notes

Brave — fullscreen / theater mode

KNOWN ISSUE (Brave v1.91.168) When using Brave with the sidebar panel open, switching from YouTube fullscreen back to theater mode may cause a layout shift: the video area becomes slightly oversized, and the ShuKage control bar may be displaced or hidden. This layout issue also occurs with Brave's own sidebar panel when ShuKage is disabled, which points to Brave-side rendering behavior rather than an extension-specific problem.

We are monitoring Brave updates for a fix before considering further workarounds.

In the meantime: hiding the sidebar panel and resizing manually restores the correct layout. Alternatively, open the video in a new window, where the issue does not occur.

Disabling the extension

Toolbar toggle vs. chrome://extensions Clicking the extension icon in the browser toolbar to turn ShuKage off is a per-site temporary toggle — reloading the page may re-enable it. To fully disable the extension, go to chrome://extensions and use the enable/disable switch. After disabling there, reload the page and the extension will remain off.

Recording destination

NOTE Recordings are saved to whichever marker is selected at the time recording stops — not necessarily the one that was active when recording started. If you switch markers during a recording session, the recording will be saved to the newly selected marker. This can be used intentionally to route recordings to a specific marker.

Switching markers resets the ▶⏺ button to its inactive state, so auto-stop no longer applies. To stop recording, either pause playback using the ⏸ button (or YouTube's own controls) — recording will stop automatically after the stop gap — or click the ⏹ REC button to stop it directly.

Tab switching during recording

NOTE ShuKage monitors the active YouTube tab. Switching tabs may interrupt B-point detection, causing the A–B loop to stop working while the video keeps playing. If this happens, return to the original tab and stop playback manually, or use the ▶⏺ button to stop auto-playback recording. As long as you stay on a single tab, this does not apply.
NOTE If you reload the extension from chrome://extensions (e.g. after updating), ShuKage may stop responding on YouTube tabs where the control bar was already loaded. Refresh those tabs to load ShuKage again.
